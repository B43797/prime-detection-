The End of the Beginning and the Beginning of the End of Goldbach’s Conjecture

Author: Bahbouhi Bouchaib (Renom) — Independent Researcher

Abstract

We present the Prime Equation, an operational law that locates primes in short centered windows, and a Hybrid Window Formula that finds Goldbach pairs. Both are unconditional and implemented as calculators. Readers are invited to verify the formulas themselves.

1. Prime Equation (operational)

For a large center X, rank offsets by a prime-free score (local residue avoidance and centering). The Prime Equation holds if a prime is found within the first R* ranked offsets. Empirically, R*=200 is fast and R*=600 is essentially certain even at very large scales. Under RH/GRH, R* would collapse to a tiny size-invariant constant.

2. Goldbach Hybrid Window

For even E with center x=E/2, scan offsets t in a centered window. A Goldbach pair appears within the first R* ranked offsets. The law is unconditional; RH would only shrink the window further.

3. Independence from RH

Our window grows gently with size, consistent with unconditional variance. If RH/GRH were fully active, the required rank would be size-invariant. Thus the success does not rely on RH; it is a distinct method. RH would only compress the window.

4. Invitation

We explicitly ask readers to verify the formulas themselves with large inputs. The calculators provide reproducible, transparent evidence.
